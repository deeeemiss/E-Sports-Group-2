Italy

The growth prospects are good and as the eSport 2019 report, presented in Milan by Aesvi (Association of Italian Italian Video Game Developers Association) and Nielsen, this new video game phenomenon is attracting a large audience of gamers.
Approximately 350,000 gamers are actively involved in esports events in our country and over one million are those who claim to follow these types of events. Unfortunately, these numbers have not yet woken up the institutions, which still see video games as something dedicated to children and teenagers locked away in their bedrooms. A problem of mentality that risks preventing esports stadium projects from taking off in our country.
To take the field, first and foremost, are companies and football teams that have formed multi-gaming teams, which participate in self-regulated competitions. Surely soon soon we will see the first esports arenas capable of concentrating a large number of gamers, and an early example was the PG nationals finals, where the Olimpico turned into a mini esports stadium. While waiting to see how the Lega Calcio Serie A will make its first official and serious move into the world of esports.
